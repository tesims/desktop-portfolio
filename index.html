Current Major Projects

Gralx - multi-modal data mask that preserves context

Gralx is an open-source project that preserves vital contextual information in multi-modal training datasets while anonymizing personally identifiable information (PII) through pseudonymization with synthetic data. Traditional data masking often removes crucial context—like race, gender, age, and emotions—essential for training accurate and unbiased AI models. Gralx allows users to upload multimedia data and specify which contexts to retain. It then generates synthetic data that maintains these important attributes but replaces sensitive identifiers. By integrating advanced models for dense video captioning, audio transcription, text-to-image generation, and image-to-video animation, Gralx creates a pipeline that produces PII-compliant datasets ready for machine learning without sacrificing essential context.

Drolg - AI party DJ by processing video feed in real-time

Drolg is a project that helps DJs choose the perfect music for their audience by analyzing crowd movements and reactions using computer vision. By processing live video feeds, the system interprets the crowd's energy and vibe—assessing factors like movement and pose—and selects songs from Spotify that match these dynamics. This ensures that the music resonates with the audience's mood in real time, enhancing their overall experience and keeping the energy high on the dance floor.
